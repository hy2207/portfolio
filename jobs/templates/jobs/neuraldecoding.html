Neural Decoding

Project Overview

This project not only investigate the relationship between single and multi-finger movements by analyzing the firing pattern of neurons, but also build a decdoing model for inferring multi-finger movements using only single finger movements.

Introduction

What is a Neural Decoding?
  • Neurons encode information in the form of an electrical signal (e.g. spike train)
  • Inferring information about motion/ sensory signal in the spike train is called neural decoding

    • Focused on motion decoding, especially decoding finger developingfor safe and convenient life

    Proposed Decoding

    To estimate multi-finger movements,

  • Conventional decoding: necessary to model various finger combinations
  • Proposed decoding: Estimate multi-finger movements requiring only single finger movements data
    • Experimental Protocol
  • Subject: Two macaca mulatta monkey training to perform visually cued individuated finger movements
  • Recording area: Task-related neurons in primary motor cortex; M1
  • Movements:
      • Monkey K: 10 single movements & 6 multi-finger movements
      • Monkey S: 10 single movements & 2 multi-finger movements
  • Number of trials: 6

    • Method

    Perievent time histogram (PETH)
  • Using PETH to identify neural activity in each motions

    • "The neurons responsive to single finger movements are also participating in corresponding multi-finger movements"

    Pearson's correlation coeffiecient


  • Using Pearson's correlation coeffiecient
  • Each circle represents the correlation, the size of circle is proportional to the correlation coefficient
  • Left figure shows the neuron-averaged correlation from the 10 high-responding neuron group
  • Showing the physically related single and combined-finger pairs, the correlation-coefficient values are high
    • Maximum Likelihood(ML)
  • Using to estimate the intended movement
  • ML Flow Chart
  • Using skellam distribution for probablistic model of neural activation
    • Proposed Decoding model
  • Estimating the multi-finger models using the neural activity obtained when the corresponding single finger movements are performed
  • Proposed method
  • Assume that the energy consumed by neurons is constant when performing any motions
  • The energy consumed in the multi-finger movement can be the average of energy consumed in performing the single finger movements
  • The estimated variance is also calculated in a similar manner with this equation

    • Conclusion

    Decoding performance
  • Quantifying the neurological correlations between single finger movements and their combined movements
  • Propose the novel neural decoding which can discriminate multi-finger movements based on independent and elementary finger movements
  • So, prosthetic hand users can expect to skip the boring training process of multi-finger movements
    • Limitation
  • Unless assuming constant energy is consumed by the neurons to estimate probability model, the evidence of this assumption has not been produced.
  • Verification in more various combinations of multi-finger movements is needed
